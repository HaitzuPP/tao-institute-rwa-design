Resources / Methodology

Methodology

Scoring framework, tool definitions, and data sources

Start with the Subnet Risk Score — the composite assessment that scores every subnet 0–100. The three primary tools (Deregistration Index, Subnet Momentum, Revenue Tracker) feed into the score's dimensions. Two secondary monitors (Validator Concentration, Alpha Flow) provide real-time risk signals between scoring updates.
Subnet Risk Score — Scoring Architecture

0–100 composite score from 13 on-chain metrics across 4 dimensions. All inputs are publicly available — no proprietary data access required. Since Bittensor's 2025 shift to flow-based emissions, allocation efficiency is the primary survival signal, which is why Emissions Economics carries the largest weight.

INVESTIBILITY TIERS
A · 80+ B · 60–79 C · 40–59 D · 20–39 F · <20

Thresholds calibrated so that historically, subnets scoring below 60 had >3× higher deregistration probability within 90 days. Tiers are quality filters, not investment recommendations.

FOUR RISK DIMENSIONS
DimensionWeightFocusMetrics
Emissions Economics40%Emission capture and retention efficiencyEmission share, trend, efficiency, stake retention
Tokenomic Sustainability25%Alpha token liquidity, price stability, miner sell pressureAlpha/TAO ratio, pool depth, sell pressure
Fundamental Sustainability20%Product-market fit and revenue potential beyond emissionsExternal revenue, API usage, customer count, code activity
Strategic Positioning15%Team strength proxy: governance, validator support, ecosystem tiesValidator count, governance score, team visibility
SCORING MECHANICS
Layer 1: Quantitative
13 metrics scored 1–5 (integer), weighted by dimension → 0–100 composite. 5 binary flags overlay: single-validator dependency, closed-source code, no emissions 30d+, anonymous team, pending deregistration.
Layer 2: Qualitative Flags
Displayed alongside the quantitative score — not baked in. Covers team credibility, competitive moat, regulatory exposure, and known data gaps that the model can't capture.
Update Cadence
Full scores updated quarterly. Binary flags and dimension sub-scores refresh with each tool update (daily for most tools).
Deregistration Index
Key Finding

Bottom-10 subnets cluster within a 6% price spread before removal. 42 implied deregistration events since June 2025, median cadence of 5 days. Entering this zone predicts removal within ~14 days.

Tracks which subnets are closest to removal. When all 128 slots are full and a new subnet registers, Bittensor removes the lowest-ranked non-immune subnet.

REMOVAL MECHANISM
StepWhat HappensKey Data
1. Slot FullAll 128 slots occupied. New subnet burns TAO to register.Slot count (128/128)
2. Pruning RankSubnets ranked by pruning score (stake + emissions + performance). Lowest identified.Pruning score, position
3. Immunity CheckIf lowest-ranked is within 7-day immunity window, skip to next-lowest.Registration timestamp
4. RemovalSubnet deregistered. Miners/validators ejected. Staked TAO returned.Deregistration event
Subnet Momentum

Tracks which subnets are gaining or losing emission share — a leading indicator of validator confidence. Post-halving emissions are fixed at 3,600τ/day, so it's a zero-sum game: one subnet's gain is another's loss.

SignalDefinitionImplication
Rising ShareEmission percentage increasing vs. network average.Validators adding weight — bullish.
Falling ShareEmission percentage decreasing. Often precedes stake outflows.Validator confidence eroding. Watch for cascading withdrawals.
Category RotationAggregate shifts at category level (AI/ML, Compute, DeFi, Data).Macro signal: capital rotating between sector themes.
Validator Concentration Index

Measures systemic risk from validator centralisation. A subnet dependent on 1–3 validators is fragile — if a major validator withdraws, the subnet can lose its emission allocation overnight. Uses the Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI): a concentration score from 0 (perfectly distributed) to 10,000 (single validator). Above 2,500 = high risk.

MetricThresholdRisk Level
Top-3 Stake Share>33%SYSTEMIC

Single-Validator Dependency>50% from 1 validatorBINARY FLAG
Delegation PatternsNet outflow >5% / weekWATCH LIST
Alpha Flow Monitor

Tracks TAO flowing into and out of subnet alpha pools over 24–72h windows. When multiple large wallets (500τ+) converge on the same subnet within a short timeframe, it's a "smart money" signal. Data from on-chain pool transactions, labelled wallets (OTF, dao5, Yuma, known funds), and pool depth snapshots at 15-minute intervals.

SIGNAL CLASSIFICATION
SignalDefinitionWhen to Use
ACCELAccelerating inflow — increasing volume over consecutive windows.Strongest conviction signal. Often precedes 7D price moves.
INFLOWSteady positive flow above baseline.Accumulation phase — confirms momentum signals.
STEADYNet flow within ±5% of 7D average.No directional signal. Hold or monitor.
OUTFLOWConsistent withdrawals below baseline.Distribution phase. Cross-check with momentum.
DRAINHeavy exits — >20% pool reduction in 7D.Capitulation signal. Check deregistration proximity.
Revenue Tracker

Measures external revenue — income from real customers, not emission subsidies. Classified as Verified (on-chain confirmed) or Estimated (inferred from API usage / team disclosures). Only 8 of 128 subnets generate meaningful external revenue.

MetricFormulaInterpretation
Revenue / τ Emitted30D external revenue ÷ total τ emissions received.Does the subnet earn enough to justify its allocation?
Emission EfficiencyDaily revenue ÷ (daily τ × TAO spot price).100% = self-sustaining. <20% = pure emission subsidy.
Revenue MultipleAlpha market cap ÷ annualised revenue.Lower = cheaper relative to revenue. Comparable to P/S ratio.
Growth-Adjusted MultipleRevenue Multiple ÷ (QoQ revenue growth%).<0.10 = market underpricing growth. Comparable to PEG.
Data Sources & Reliability
DATA SOURCES
On-chain: Taostats API, subtensor chain queries
Revenue: API monitoring, team disclosures, on-chain tx analysis
Validators: Delegation records, stake snapshots
Alpha pools: dTAO pool depth, alpha/TAO exchange rates
Cadence: Scores quarterly, tools daily
WHAT TO DISTRUST
Deregistration Index: Directional only — 2 observed removals limits precision. Don't use alone for low-conviction calls.
Revenue data: 120 subnets self-report or report nothing. Treat non-verified revenue as estimates.
Alpha Flow labels: Wallet labelling is probabilistic. "OTF Wallet" = high confidence; unlabelled addresses = moderate.
Historical depth: Time-series start June 2025. Pre-dTAO data unavailable.
Pruning rank: EMA parameters undocumented by OTF. Our model reverse-engineers from observed outcomes.
